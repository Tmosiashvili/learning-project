# Video Info
- Title: Mastering Messaging for B2B Tech with Emma Stratton
- Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L8YFy06-eRM 
- Creator: April Dunford (host), Emma Stratton (guest)
- Topic: Difference between positioning, messaging, and copywriting + how to write clear B2B messaging
## Main Ideas
- Positioning = strategy (who, what, why)
- Messaging = translation of strategy into consistent language
- Copywriting = execution across channels
- Jargon reduces clarity, not increases credibility
- Specific plain-language improves understanding
- Audience definition is required before messaging
- Buyer awareness level changes messaging strategy
- Headlines must earn attention, not summarize everything
- “Platform” is overused and meaningless
- Specificity is the core driver of differentiation
## Key Insights
- Curse of knowledge is the root cause of bad messaging
- “Imagine if you could…” forces customer-centric thinking
- Messaging layer is missing in most B2B orgs
- Narrowing audience is the hardest but most important step
- CPG background improves B2B clarity and discipline
## Psychological / Marketing Angle
- Curse of knowledge reduces defensiveness and increases acceptance
- Specificity creates mental imagery → better memory
- Before/after structure makes improvement obvious
- Mutual credibility between host and guest builds trust
## SaaS / Business Application
- Audit headlines for clarity test (standalone understanding)
- Replace jargon with plain English everywhere
- Align messaging to buyer awareness stage
- Build champion-friendly “plain English” pitch
- Separate positioning, messaging, and copywriting work explicitly
## Short Summary
Most B2B companies fail at messaging because they collapse positioning, messaging, and copywriting into one process. The missing layer is messaging — the translation step — and the main blocker is the curse of knowledge, which makes internal teams unable to judge clarity from an outsider perspective.
