# Kyle Poyar — LinkedIn Posts
## Post 1 — AI-Native SaaS Disruption
Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kyle-poyar_lightfieldpartner-activity-7452359845829177344-LdNU  
Date:1 week ago  
## Hook
“SaaS stocks are down 20%… lowest multiples in a decade. WTF does this mean for legacy SaaS?”
## Main Idea
Traditional SaaS is being disrupted by AI-native products that remove friction entirely — not just add features.
## Key Insight
The disruption is not AI features, but elimination of user effort.  
AI-native SaaS turns systems of record into systems of execution.
## Psychological Triggers
- Fear (market decline)
- Contrast (old vs new SaaS model)
- Curiosity gap (“WTF does this mean?”)
- Inevitability framing
## Why it works
- Macro tension → immediate urgency
- Simple analogy (newspapers vs SaaS)
- Clear breakdown of shift
- Strong closing “irreversible insight”
## SaaS Application
- Position product as category shift, not improvement
- Emphasize “removal of work” instead of features
- Use old vs new paradigm storytelling
- Start from market disruption → zoom into product value
## Summary
Kyle frames AI-native SaaS as a structural shift where value moves from software that stores data to software that executes work automatically.
## Post 2 — Growth Ownership in SaaS
Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kyle-poyar_who-owns-growth-heres-what-the-latest-data-activity-7444735295796838400-QiS6  
Date: 1 month ago  
## Hook
“Who owns growth? Here’s what the latest data shows 👀”
## Main Idea
Growth in SaaS is fragmented across teams, leading to unclear ownership and accountability.
## Key Insight
The main bottleneck in SaaS growth is not tactics — it is **organizational misalignment across the funnel**.
## Psychological Triggers
- Authority (data-backed analysis)
- Validation (“this confusion is normal”)
- Role tension (marketing vs product vs sales)
- Engagement through open question
## Why it works
- Data increases credibility
- Clear structure improves readability
- Highlights internal contradictions
- Mix of data + opinion builds trust
- Ending question boosts engagement
## SaaS Application
- Use data to reveal hidden org problems
- Position product as solution to misalignment
- Segment insights by team (marketing, sales, product)
- Combine analytics with interpretation
- End posts with provocative questions
## Summary
Growth ownership problems in SaaS are primarily structural, not tactical, and stem from unclear accountability across teams.
