## Post 1
Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/sangramvajre_in-my-ceo-group-call-i-asked-whats-your-activity-7447987939436969984-HzIy  
Date: 3 weeks ago  
## Hook
“In my CEO group call, I asked: ‘What’s your revenue motion?’  
A lot of them said: ‘We do a little bit of everything.’  
That’s a BIG problem.”
## Main Idea
Every B2B company runs on 6 revenue motions, but most companies don’t manage them intentionally:
1. Inbound-Led — demand generated via content and digital channels  
2. Outbound-Led — targeted sales + account-based outreach  
3. Product-Led — product drives adoption and conversion  
4. Partner-Led — ecosystem-driven growth (referrals, affiliates, resellers)  
5. Event-Led — in-person or virtual events that create pipeline  
6. Community-Led — thought leadership and long-term audience building  
Most companies use 2–3 of these, but without strategic ownership.
## Key Insight
A real revenue motion is not just activity. It is:
 Segment + ICP + Product + GTM Motion + Ownership
Without this full structure, companies are just running disconnected activities instead of a unified growth system.
## Psychological Trigger
- Pattern interruption (“we do everything” = red flag)
- Authority (CEO group + GTM Partners research)
- Simplification (6 clear motions)
- Diagnostic framing (forces self-evaluation)
- Identity tension (most companies realize they are “unstructured”)
## Why It Works
- Turns messy GTM execution into a clean framework  
- Helps companies self-diagnose instantly  
- Provides operational clarity (what to own vs what to ignore)  
- Shifts thinking from tactics → system design  
## SaaS / Business Application
- Audit your GTM: identify which of the 6 motions actually drives revenue  
- Assign clear ownership per motion  
- Stop mixing motions without strategy  
- Build reporting around motion performance, not channel performance  
- Align ICP + motion (each motion serves different buyer dynamics)
## Short Summary
Most B2B companies fail in GTM because they run multiple revenue motions without structure or ownership. Sangram defines six core motions, but emphasizes that real revenue strategy only exists when each motion is tied to ICP, product, and ownership.
## Post 2
Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/sangramvajre_salesforce-17-years-to-go-from-1b-to-9b-activity-7432054214685732864-q5eE  
Date: 2 months ago  
## Hook
“Salesforce: 17 years to go from $1B to $9B.  
Anthropic: 10 months.  
That’s not a stat. That’s a reset.”
## Main Idea
The software industry is shifting from:
Software as a Service (SaaS) → Service as a Software
Old model:
- Buy software  
- Hire people to operate it  
- Hope it delivers outcomes  
New model:
- Hire experts who bring tools + execution  
- Focus on outcomes, not software usage  
## Key Insight
- Traditional SaaS is losing its monopoly on execution  
- The product itself is no longer the moat — outcomes are  
- AI-native companies scale faster because the software does the work  
## Psychological Trigger
- Shock comparison (Salesforce vs Anthropic speed gap)  
- Narrative disruption (SaaS era vs AI era)  
- Fear of obsolescence  
- Identity shift in GTM roles  
## Why It Works
- Extreme contrast creates urgency  
- Reframes entire industry in one idea  
- Signals structural change, not incremental update  
## SaaS / Business Application
- Sell outcomes, not features  
- Reduce dependency on human execution  
- Combine software + expertise  
- GTM becomes hybrid (product + service layer)  
- Measure success by results, not usage  
## Short Summary
SaaS is shifting from software delivery to outcome delivery. AI accelerates this change by embedding execution into the product itself. The competitive advantage moves from features to outcomes.
