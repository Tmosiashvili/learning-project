## Post Overview
- Source: Lenny Rachitsky (interview insights from Anthropic Head of Growth – Amol Avasare)
- Topic: How AI is reshaping growth, product management, and cross-functional work inside Anthropic
- Focus: AI-driven org design, growth automation, and the evolving role of PMs
## Main Ideas
- Engineering is massively amplified by AI
  - Claude Code enables small engineering teams to output 3–4x more work
  - This creates imbalance: engineering scales faster than PM/design
- PM role is evolving, not disappearing
  - One PM now effectively manages output of much larger engineering teams
  - Anthropic responds by:
    - Hiring more PMs
    - Turning engineers into “mini-PMs” for small projects
- AI is already automating growth work internally
  - Internal system: CASH (Claude Accelerates Sustainable Hypergrowth)
  - Automates:
    - Opportunity discovery
    - Feature building
    - Testing
    - Analysis
  - Performance ≈ junior PM (2–3 years experience)
- Hardest PM problem remains human alignment
  - AI cannot solve stakeholder disagreement
  - “Getting 6 people in a room to agree” remains bottleneck
- Most growth work happens without PRDs
  - 60–80% of projects start in Slack, not formal documentation
  - Only larger initiatives use structured kickoffs (legal, design, safeguards)
- Strategic onboarding friction improves conversion
  - Adding intentional friction helps users understand product value
  - Removing “annoying friction” is good — but meaningful friction improves clarity
- AI agents used for internal misalignment detection
  - Weekly Slack-scanning agent identifies:
    - Overlapping work
    - Cross-team misalignment
  - Prevents wasted engineering effort and coordination issues
## Key Insights
- AI increases output, but exposes coordination bottlenecks
  - Engineering scales faster than human alignment structures
- Growth is becoming partially automated
  - AI can now handle early-stage PM + growth execution tasks
- Human coordination becomes more valuable, not less
  - Alignment, politics, and decision-making remain human-centric constraints
- Lightweight workflows are replacing heavy documentation
  - Slack-first execution > PRD-first execution for small work
- Onboarding design is shifting from “frictionless” to “intentional friction”
  - The goal is understanding, not just speed
## Psychological / Marketing Angle
- Automation anxiety vs empowerment
  - Fear: PM roles shrinking
  - Reality: PM role shifting upward into coordination and strategy
- Authority effect
  - Insights from Anthropic (top-tier AI company) increase credibility
- Cognitive dissonance
  - “AI writes code like 15 engineers” vs “humans still can’t align 6 people”
- Pattern disruption
  - “No PRD for 80% of work” challenges traditional product management norms
##  SaaS / Business Application
- Use AI to automate repetitive growth experiments
- Redesign PM workflows to be Slack-first for small tasks
- Invest in cross-functional alignment systems
- Introduce intentional onboarding friction to improve activation quality
- Build internal AI agents for:
  - Slack monitoring
  - workflow overlap detection
  - growth opportunity discovery
##  Short Summary
Anthropic’s growth team shows that AI is already automating significant parts of product and growth work, especially execution-heavy tasks like feature testing and analysis. However, human coordination—alignment across teams, decision-making, and prioritization—remains the hardest and least automatable bottleneck. The future of PM and growth roles shifts from execution to orchestration, while AI handles much of the tactical workload.
