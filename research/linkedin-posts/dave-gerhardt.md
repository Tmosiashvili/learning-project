## Post 1 - CMO Research Insights (CORE)

Link: [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_what-100-b2b-cmos-said-they-are-worried-about-share-7455582368423215105-MMm1/](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_what-100-b2b-cmos-said-they-are-worried-about-share-7455582368423215105-MMm1/)  
Date: 2 days ago  

## Summary:

This post summarizes research from 100 CMOs at $50M–$500M ARR companies, identifying their top current concerns.

## Key Insights:

- CMOs are under pressure to prove revenue impact beyond MQLs  
- AI adoption is expected to deliver practical ROI, not experimentation  
- Marketing teams are under-resourced while being asked to deliver more with less  
- Shift in language from MQLs to pipeline quality, revenue contribution, retention

## Why it matters:

This is a primary research-based post that captures a real shift in B2B SaaS marketing from lead generation thinking to revenue-driven marketing models.

## Post 2 — Lifecycle Marketing Strategy (SUPPORTING)

Link: [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_the-marketing-playbook-for-keeping-customers-activity-7454854442874568704-ob3s](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_the-marketing-playbook-for-keeping-customers-activity-7454854442874568704-ob3s)  
Date: 4 days ago  

## Summary:

This post argues that SaaS companies over-invest in acquisition while under-investing in customer retention and lifecycle marketing.

## Key Insights:

- Retention marketing is underused compared to acquisition marketing  
- Customer engagement should be treated as a marketing channel, not just CS function  
- Lifecycle marketing creates compounding returns vs one-time acquisition spend  
- Strong SaaS teams integrate post-sale communication into marketing strategy

## Why it matters:

It reframes marketing responsibility beyond acquisition and highlights expansion/retention as a growth lever.
